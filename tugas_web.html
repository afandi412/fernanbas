Riset indicate that more than 75% of all school-age minors are affected by social media, but at one point or another in the world (source).It's also the most difficult to catch and prosecute, as the anonymous nature of the internet can lead to threats coming from anywhere and from everyone who allows people the opportunity to do stuff they might never do in real life, as they can't threaten people face to face, or they can't get in trouble as easily,And the results of this can have an effect on the person being bullied terribly because they feel they are defenseless and have no way to combat the people who hurt them.Humans live in a world where people are being humiliated and abused. According to an anonymous report (2014), "Cyberbullying happens for many of the same reasons as any other type of bullying, but it may be even more appealing because it can be done anonymously". Cyberbullying is an act of using social media technologies to threaten others that are done anonymously in the main cases. By using cellphones or some other tool, such as computers and tablets, to connect to the Internet, a vast amount of people have spent their time bullying other people. Cyberbullying may not harm anyone physically; it may affect them psychologically instead.

One of the key reasons Cyber-Bullying is triggered is the open existence of the internet, as said before. Studies reveal in a quote to Kowalski, Giumetti, Shroeder, and Latanner "They find that cyberbullies felt invisible, which gives them more motivation to be violent because they know they are alone, does not have any repercussions. With the internet, you can use that secret and mystical aspect to make a person feel strong, untouchable even though they believe they can't be harmed by someone or something, so no one can ever figure out who or where they are, hence never getting into trouble. They strike in terms that are far worse than they can in normal life, and that can hurt even more than any actual altercation therein.

On social media platforms such as Facebook, Myspace, and Twitter, cyberbullying will take place. "By 2008, 93% of young people aged 12 to 17 were online (source). Also, young people spend more time on the internet than any other activity except sleeping."The last decade has witnessed an increase in cyberbullying, which is categorized as bullying by using electronic communications technologies such as Email, instant messaging, social media, online gaming, or digital messages or images sent to a cell phone.Eight out of ten teens who use social media now share more information about themselves than they have in the past, according to a 2013 study by Pew Research. It includes their location, pictures, and contact information. It is important to keep personal information such as age, birthday, school/church, telephone number, etc. confidential to protect the children.Two 2014 reports showed that 80 percent of body-shaming tweets were sent by women, while they accounted for 50% of misogynistic tweets, too (Pew Research, 2014).Cyberbullying can also occur by the use of websites belonging to other individuals to effectively recommend that another person or group be targeted.
